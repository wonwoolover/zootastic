Understanding the World of Crocodiles

What are "Crocodiles"?

Crocodiles are large reptiles with powerful jaws, armored skin, and incredible hunting skills. They are among the oldest living animals on Earth, having existed for over 200 million years

How do crocodiles communicate?

Crocodiles communicate through growls, hisses, grunts, and body movements. Female crocodiles make soft calls to their hatchlings, while males use loud bellows to attract mates. Vibrations in the water also send signals over long distances.

When Did Crocodiles First Appear?

Crocodiles appeared over 200 million years ago and have changed very little, making them living fossils. Their ancient adaptations such as strong jaws, stealth, and armored skin‚Äîhave allowed them to survive mass extinctions.

The Saltwater Crocodile
The Largest Living Reptile

A Fascinating Look Into The Mighty Saltwater Crocodile

The Saltwater Crocodile is the largest crocodile species and the largest living reptile. They prefer warm climates and spend much of their time partially submerged, waiting to ambush prey. Their presence indicates a functioning, balanced ecosystem.
Despite being named for their ocean habitat, they frequently travel far upstream in fresh water. They are found from Northern Australia to the eastern coast of India and Southeast Asia. Crocodiles eat fish, birds, reptiles, amphibians, and mammals. Large crocodiles can take down big animals like zebras and wildebeests.

The Orinoco Crocodile
Native to the Orinoco River Basin in Colombia

The Orinoco Crocodile: Disappearing by Historical Hunting

The Orinoco Crocodile is Critically Endangered. It is one of the world's most threatened crocodilians, endemic to a restricted river system in South America.

It has been hunted over three generations (approximately 75 years), largely driven by catastrophic commercial hunting for its skin in the 20th century.

Why Are Orinoco crocodile Endangered?

The primary cause was over-hunting for the leather trade. Today, surviving populations are threatened by illegal killing, pollution, and competition with other species.

What Threatens Crocodiles Habitats?

The remaining river and savanna habitats are being degraded by pollution from mining and agricultural runoff, and the construction of dams which alters their natural flood cycles.
