Eco-friendly practices zookeepers
should foster in order to get in more visitors

"Revitalization starts with us. Start small, dream big."

Take good care of animals

Prepare them enough food and nutritious diets while monitoring each animal every month or so. Animals are the face of the zoo parks. We must all conserve them, especially the endangered ones and that starts with the zookeepers. The zookeepers should clean their habitats as well as no visitors will come if they have dirt and smell.

Regularly clean areas

Pest management practices include regularly clean ups to remove food residues that attract pests. Additionally, cleaning up everytime there is a large number of visitors is also very impactful as it gives the impression to the visitors that the zoo park is clean and well-managed.

Encourage visitors engagement

Visitors seem to be isolated unless they come with a group so they should adopt practices such as preparing fun games or making the zoo park lively again with a variety of tools.

Practices for monkeys

Did you know monkeys scream?

Monkeys communicate using facial expressions, body language, sounds, and sometimes touch. They can scream to warn others about danger, and use specific gestures to show emotions like fear.

  • Feed the monkeys
  • Do not disturb them
  • Dance with the monkeys

Practices for tigers

Did you know tigers are big kitties?

Tigers are the largest members of the cat family and are known for their iconic orange fur with black stripes. They are powerful predators with strong jaws, sharp claws, and exceptional hunting abilities. Each tiger has a unique stripe pattern, similar to a human fingerprint.

  • Do not take pictures with flash
  • Do not tease tigers
  • Stay away from tigers' cages

Practices for elephants

Elephants are extremely important?

Elephants are vital "ecosystem engineers". By pushing down trees and spreading seeds through their dung, they help maintain savannas and clear forest pathways.

  • Feed them a large portion of food
  • Do not throw food at them
  • Do not be scared of elephants

Practices for otters

Otters are endangered currently!

Many otter species are endangered because of water pollution, habitat destruction, hunting, and declining fish populations. Oil spills especially threaten sea otters, whose fur loses warmth when coated in oil.

  • Make squeaky sounds to them
  • Do not litter in their homes
  • Do not shout in front of them
