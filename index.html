Discover More Tips
Grow native plants

Zoos should grow native flowers and plants such as jasmine, yellow trumpetbush, desert rose, and orchids to increase long-life, decrease volume of water and lower maintainence.

Leave no trace

Visitors who come to the park should collect their own trash and not litter. The zoos should have rules concerning the matter to provide better home for animals as well as photo opportunities.

Provide labeled recycling

The zoos should display trash cans with clear labels; for instance, a bin for plastics, another bin for glass, etc. This way, the zoo can easily empty the trash and promote cleanliness as well as beauty.

What do we offer?


Our website is created to educate, inspire, and guide visitors through the natural beauty and wildlife of Yangon Zoo while promoting environmental responsibility.

We provide detailed animal knowledge, including habitats, behaviors, diets, and conservation importance. This helps visitors understand not only what animals they are seeing, but why protecting them matters.

In addition, we share eco-friendly tips and sustainable lifestyle practices that visitors can apply both inside and outside the zoo, encouraging long-term environmental awareness.

Lastly, we present thoughtful suggestions and improvement ideas aimed at enhancing animal welfare, visitor experience, and the overall sustainability of Yangon Zoo.

What else do we offer?


Our platform goes beyond information by offering interactive and user-focused features designed to keep visitors engaged.

By creating an account, users gain access to zoo event calendars, special programs, upcoming exhibitions, and important announcements.

These features help visitors stay updated on conservation efforts, educational activities, and community-driven initiatives happening within the zoo.

We also encourage feedback and participation, allowing users to share ideas, suggestions, and opinions that can contribute to positive change.

Through these tools, we aim to build a connected, informed, and environmentally conscious community that supports wildlife protection and sustainable tourism.
